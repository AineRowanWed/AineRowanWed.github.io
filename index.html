The chapel of Trinity College Dublin was chosen because of Áine's love for the college from her time spent studying and living on campus. Trinity is a special campus, right in the heart of vibrant Dublin city centre, with a beautiful and historic setting.

The chapel is an Anglican chapel in which Christian events of all denominations take place. The wedding will be a Catholic wedding ceremony, presided by Fr. Peter Sexton, the Catholic chaplain of Trinity, with whom Áine has a long connection since her days in college.

How to get there

Travelling to Ireland from abroad

You can take the plane or the ferry. If flying, please travel to Dublin airport. Ryanair or Aer Lingus are likely to be the most convenient airlines for your journey. You can also drive and take the ferry from Holyhead to Dublin.

Travelling from Dublin airport to Dublin city centre

Please use Google Maps to find the quickest journey from Dublin airport to your accommodation or to Dublin city centre.

The bus is the best form of public transport from Dublin airport to Dublin city centre:

  • Dublin Express 782 (28 minutes, 2 minutes walk to The Westin hotel)
  • Dublin Express 784 (20 minutes, 5 minutes walk to The Westin hotel)

The AirCoach 700 and 703 bus will take you door-to-door from Dublin Airport to the house of Áine's parents and The Maldron Merrion Road hotel.

Alternatively, you can take a taxi.

Travelling around Dublin

Popular modes of public transport in Dublin include:

  • Dublin City bus. Buses run very frequently. You need to pay with exact change, or else have a leapcard: Leap Visitor Card.
  • DART: this is the green train that runs along the coastline, north and south from Dublin city centre. Can pay by credit card or Leap Card.
  • LUAS: a tram. Unlikely to be useful for your visit to Dublin, even though it does pass right outside The Westin Hotel on the green line.

What else should I do in Dublin

Recommended things to do in Dublin City Centre

  • Visit The Guinness Storehouse.
  • Explore Trinity College Dublin, wherein you will find the Book of Kells and the Long Room.
  • Explore Grafton Street, and all its side streets for shopping and browsing.
  • Visit St. Stephen's Green.

Recommended things to do outside of Dublin city centre

  • Travel on the Dart along South Dublin's coastline.
    • Go swimming at Seapoint, the 40 foot or at Dalkey.
    • Walk along the coastline from Seapoint to the 40 foot.
    • Enjoy the coffee shops.
  • Travel on the Dart along North Dublin's coastline.
    • Walk Howth Head and enjoy Howth.

Recommended things to do around Ireland

  • Explore Wicklow, the county just south of Dublin. Visit Glendalough, hike in the mountains and admire the scenery.
  • Follow the Wild Atlantic Way, driving, or cycling, along the west coast of Ireland.
  • Explore Kerry, a beautiful county in the south west of Ireland. Particularly, we recommend Killarney national parks, the Gap of Dunloe, the Ring of Kerry drive, all the beaches along the coastline, and climbing Carauntoohill (Ireland's highest mountain).
